## Medical Bill Disputes in Wisconsin: Your Rights and Options

Wisconsin patients dealing with medical billing errors or unexpected charges have access to both federal protections and state insurance oversight. The Wisconsin Office of the Commissioner of Insurance actively handles consumer complaints and provides free assistance to residents.

## Key Wisconsin Consumer Protections

### Federal No Surprises Act (2022)

The federal law protects most Wisconsin patients with private insurance from balance billing for emergency services and from out-of-network providers at in-network facilities. Wisconsin relies primarily on the federal framework for surprise billing protection rather than a separate comprehensive state law.

### Wisconsin Insurance Code Protections

Wisconsin's insurance statutes (Wis. Stat. Chapters 600–646) require health insurers to maintain grievance processes, respond to consumer complaints, and process claims within required timeframes. The Wisconsin Office of the Commissioner of Insurance (OCI) enforces these requirements and provides free consumer assistance.

### Wisconsin Hospital Financial Assistance

Wisconsin nonprofit hospitals are required by IRS rules to have charity care policies. Major Wisconsin health systems including Aurora Health Care (Advocate Aurora), Aspirus Health, Froedtert Health, and UW Health have financial assistance programs for qualifying patients. Wisconsin has a strong tradition of community healthcare and access programs.

### BadgerCare Plus (Wisconsin Medicaid)

Wisconsin's BadgerCare Plus program provides Medicaid coverage to qualifying residents. BadgerCare members have separate billing protections and dispute processes through their managed care plan. Wisconsin has partial Medicaid expansion, and income limits vary by category.

## Who Regulates Medical Billing in Wisconsin

### Wisconsin Office of the Commissioner of Insurance (OCI)

OCI regulates health insurance companies in Wisconsin, handles consumer complaints, and provides free insurance counseling to Wisconsin residents through the Wisconsin Insurance Plan (WIP) and the Wisconsin Senior Health Insurance Assistance Program (SHIP).

- **Website:** oci.wi.gov
- **Consumer Hotline:** 1-800-236-8517
- **File a Complaint:** oci.wi.gov/Pages/Consumers/FilingAComplaint.aspx

### Wisconsin Department of Justice – Consumer Protection

For deceptive or fraudulent billing practices.

- **Website:** doj.state.wi.us/consumer-protection
- **Consumer Hotline:** 1-800-422-7128

### Wisconsin Department of Health Services (DHS)

For Medicaid (BadgerCare Plus) billing complaints.

- **Website:** dhs.wisconsin.gov
- **ForwardHealth Member Helpline:** 1-800-362-3002

## How to Dispute a Medical Bill in Wisconsin

**Step 1: Get your itemized bill.** Request a complete, line-by-line itemized bill. Use our [EOB decoder](/eob-decoder) to decode your insurance explanation.

**Step 2: Identify errors.** Look for duplicate charges, upcoding, phantom services, and balance billing problems. See our [billing errors guide](/billing-errors) for a full list.

**Step 3: Write to the provider.** Send a formal written dispute identifying each specific charge you are challenging. Request a billing hold during the dispute.

**Step 4: File a grievance with your insurer.** Wisconsin-regulated plans must have internal grievance procedures. File in writing and document all responses.

**Step 5: File with OCI.** If the insurer does not resolve the dispute, file a consumer complaint with OCI. The office investigates and can require corrective action. OCI also provides free informal counseling to help you understand your rights.

**Step 6: External review.** Wisconsin has external review procedures for denied claims. Ask OCI about eligibility.

## FAQ

**Q: Does OCI offer free assistance to Wisconsin residents navigating billing disputes?**
A: Yes. OCI provides free informal assistance and counseling to Wisconsin residents with insurance complaints. Call their consumer hotline or file an online complaint for assistance.

**Q: Are Wisconsin nonprofit hospitals required to publicize their financial assistance programs?**
A: Yes. Under IRS Section 501(r), nonprofit hospitals must have widely publicized financial assistance policies. Ask the hospital's financial counselor about the application process and income thresholds.

**Q: How quickly must Wisconsin insurers pay or deny a claim?**
A: Wisconsin insurance regulations require insurers to acknowledge claims promptly and to pay or deny within specific timeframes (generally 30–45 days for clean claims). Late payments can trigger complaints with OCI.

**Q: What should I do if a Wisconsin provider threatens to send my bill to collections while I am disputing it?**
A: Send the provider a formal written dispute immediately (certified mail, return receipt). Under federal guidance, disputed bills should not be sent to collections until the dispute is resolved. If the provider persists, contact OCI and the Wisconsin Department of Justice.

**Q: Are Wisconsin medical debt collection practices regulated?**
A: Wisconsin follows federal FDCPA protections. Additionally, Wisconsin has the Wisconsin Consumer Act (WCA) which provides state-level consumer protections in debt collection situations. Contact Legal Action of Wisconsin if you believe a collector is violating your rights.
